Trenton's Japanese Learning Methods

Immerse Yourself Early

Trenton recommends immersing yourself in Japanese as soon as possible, even before mastering grammar. Watch anime, listen to music, and surround yourself with native content to develop a natural "feel" for the language.

Don't Focus on Textbooks

Rather than sticking to traditional textbooks, Trenton suggests learning through real-world media and context. Use grammar and vocabulary resources only to clarify things as they come up in content you consume.

Learn to Read Early

He stresses learning Hiragana and Katakana early, and gradually reading Japanese books, manga, and websites, even if you don’t understand everything at first.

  • Use Tofugu's Hiragana Guide
  • Start reading with Tadoku graded readers

Use Anki with Caution

Trenton does use Anki but emphasizes it shouldn't dominate your study. Focus more on natural exposure and meaningful context than obsessing over flashcards.

Speak When You’re Ready

He believes it’s okay to delay speaking until you feel ready. Don't rush into output if you’re still building comprehension. Internalize first, then speak naturally.

Consistency Over Intensity

Trenton encourages building habits and routines over intense cramming. Daily small steps are more effective than big bursts followed by burnout.

Trust the Process

Learning a language takes time. Trust the immersion method, and don't get discouraged if progress feels slow — consistency pays off.
